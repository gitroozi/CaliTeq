# Calisthenics Exercise Progression Trees - Complete Reference

**Purpose:** Comprehensive progression pathways for all 22 movement patterns in the CaliFlow app

**How to Use This Document:**
- Each pattern has a progression tree from beginner (Difficulty 1) to expert (Difficulty 10)
- Prerequisites are listed for each progression step
- Time estimates show typical duration to master each level
- Equipment needs are specified
- Safety notes highlight injury risks and contraindications

**Difficulty Scale:**
- **1-3:** Beginner (accessible to sedentary/untrained individuals)
- **4-6:** Intermediate (requires 3-6 months consistent training)
- **7-8:** Advanced (requires 1-2 years consistent training)
- **9-10:** Expert/Elite (requires 2+ years focused training)

**Key Exercise Details:**

**Wall Push-ups (Diff 1)**
- Stand arm's length from wall, hands at shoulder height
- Lean in, bend elbows, push back
- Equipment: Wall
- Prerequisites: None
- Safety: Ideal for absolute beginners, post-injury rehab
- Time to master: 2-4 weeks

**Standard Push-ups (Diff 5)**
- Hands shoulder-width, body straight from head to heels
- Lower chest to floor/1-2" above
- Equipment: None
- Prerequisites: 3x10 knee push-ups
- Safety: Neutral cervical spine (look 6-12" ahead, not down)
- Common mistakes: Sagging hips, flared elbows, incomplete ROM
- Time to master: 8-12 weeks from beginner

**Diamond Push-ups (Diff 7)**
- Hands together forming diamond with thumbs and index fingers
- Tricep-dominant variation
- Equipment: None
- Prerequisites: 3x15 standard push-ups
- Safety: Increased elbow stress, warm up thoroughly
- Time to master: 8-12 weeks

**One-Arm Push-ups (Diff 10)**
- One hand on ground, other behind back or extended to side
- Feet wider for stability
- Equipment: None
- Prerequisites: 3x8 archer push-ups per side
- Safety: High shoulder stress, master archer push-ups first
- Time to master: 16-24+ months total from beginner

## 2. Vertical Push {#vertical-push}

### Pike Push-up to Handstand Push-up Progression

```
Wall-Supported Pike (hands and feet on ground, inverted V) (Diff 3)
    ↓ 3x12 reps, 3-4 weeks
Pike Push-ups (Diff 4)
    ↓ 3x12 reps, 4-6 weeks
Elevated Pike Push-ups (feet on box, 12-18") (Diff 5)
    ↓ 3x10 reps, 6-8 weeks
Elevated Pike Push-ups (feet on box, 24-36") (Diff 6)
    ↓ 3x8 reps, 8-12 weeks
Wall Handstand Hold (Diff 6)
    ↓ 3x30-60 sec holds, 4-8 weeks
Wall Handstand Push-ups - Partial ROM (Diff 7)
    ↓ 3x6 reps, 8-12 weeks
Wall Handstand Push-ups - Full ROM (head to floor) (Diff 8)
    ↓ 3x8 reps, 12-16 weeks
Freestanding Handstand Hold (Diff 9)
    ↓ 60+ sec hold, 16-24 weeks
Freestanding Handstand Push-ups (Diff 10)
    ↓ Elite level, 24-36+ months
```

### Alternative: Controlled Overhead Press Progression

```
Incline Press Position (60-75 degrees) (Diff 4)
    ↓ Similar angle to pike but less loading
Steep Incline Press (75-80 degrees) (Diff 5)
    ↓
Wall Slides (scapular stability focus) (Diff 3)
    → Supports overhead pressing
```

**Key Exercise Details:**

**Pike Push-ups (Diff 4)**
- Start in downward dog position (inverted V)
- Bend elbows, lower head toward floor between hands
- Push back up
- Equipment: None
- Prerequisites: 3x12 standard push-ups
- Safety: Cervical-safe (head in line with spine, not hyperextended)
- Time to master: 4-6 weeks

**Wall Handstand Push-ups (Diff 7-8)**
- Kick up to handstand against wall, stomach facing wall
- Lower head to floor (or deficit platform for full ROM)
- Press back up
- Equipment: Wall, optional deficit platform/parallettes
- Prerequisites: 3x8 elevated pike push-ups, 60 sec wall handstand hold
- Safety: **CONTRAINDICATED for cervical disc issues** - requires medical clearance
- Spotter recommended for first attempts
- Time to master: 12-16 weeks from pike push-ups

**Freestanding Handstand Push-ups (Diff 10)**
- Handstand without wall support, perform push-ups
- Requires exceptional balance and shoulder strength
- Equipment: None (optional parallettes for ROM)
- Prerequisites: 60+ sec freestanding handstand, 3x10 wall HSPU
- Safety: Master balance first, use mats/crash pad
- Time to master: 24-36+ months total journey

### Branch B: Front Lever Progression (Advanced Skill)

```
Inverted Rows mastered (Diff 4)
    ↓

    ↓ 3x10-20 sec, 8-12 weeks
Advanced Tuck Front Lever (Diff 7)
    ↓ 3x15-20 sec, 8-12 weeks

    ↓ Elite level, 5-10 sec hold

    ↓ Beyond elite
```

**Key Exercise Details:**

**Bent-Knee Inverted Rows (Diff 2)**
- Lie under table/bar at hip height
- Knees bent 90 degrees, feet flat
- Pull chest to bar, elbows close to body
- Equipment: Table, bar, or rings
- Prerequisites: None (beginner-friendly)
- Safety: Look straight up (neutral neck), not back at feet
- Time to master: 3-4 weeks

**Straight-Leg Inverted Rows (Diff 4)**
- Same as bent-knee but legs straight, heels on ground
- Body forms plank position
- Equipment: Bar/table at hip-to-waist height
- Prerequisites: 3x12 bent-knee rows
- Safety: Maintain plank (no sagging hips)
- Time to master: 6-8 weeks

**Front Lever Hold (Diff 10)**
- Hang from bar, body horizontal and parallel to ground
- Hold position (static strength)
- Equipment: Pull-up bar
- Prerequisites: 3x8 straddle front lever holds
- Safety: Extremely high lat and core demand, progress slowly
- Time to master: 2-3+ years from beginner

**Grip Variations (applies to all levels):**
- **Overhand (pronated):** Standard, hardest
- **Underhand (supinated/chin-up):** Easier, more bicep emphasis
- **Neutral (palms facing each other):** Most shoulder-friendly, **recommended for cervical/shoulder issues**
- **Wide grip:** Lat emphasis
- **Close grip:** Bicep and mid-back emphasis

**Key Exercise Details:**

**Active Hang (Diff 2)**
- Hang from bar, pull shoulder blades down and back
- Shoulders away from ears (scapular depression)
- Equipment: Pull-up bar
- Prerequisites: Dead hang 30+ seconds
- Safety: Essential foundation for safe pulling
- Time to master: 2-3 weeks

**Negative Pull-ups (Diff 5)**
- Jump or step up to chin-over-bar position
- Lower slowly and controlled (5+ seconds)
- Equipment: Pull-up bar, box/step for jumping
- Prerequisites: 3x8 band-assisted pull-ups
- Safety: **Don't look up at bar (cervical strain)**, look forward
- Time to master: 6-8 weeks

**Full Pull-ups (Diff 6)**
- Dead hang to chin over bar
- Pull shoulder blades down first, then pull with arms
- Equipment: Pull-up bar
- Prerequisites: 3x5 negative pull-ups OR 3x10 light band-assisted
- Safety: Neutral neck (look forward), controlled descent
- Time to master: 8-12 weeks from zero
- **Milestone achievement for most users**

**One-Arm Pull-ups (Diff 10)**
- Single arm hang to chin over bar
- Ultimate vertical pull strength
- Equipment: Pull-up bar
- Prerequisites: 3x8 archer pull-ups, 1x20+ two-arm pull-ups
- Safety: Extremely high shoulder stress, very slow progression
- Time to master: 2-3+ years from first pull-up

**Key Exercise Details:**

**Box Squats (Diff 2)**
- Stand in front of sturdy box/chair (knee height)
- Lower until sitting on box, stand back up
- Equipment: Box, chair, or bench
- Prerequisites: None
- Safety: Removes fear of "getting stuck," teaches proper depth
- Time to master: 3-4 weeks

**Bodyweight Squats (Diff 3)**
- Feet shoulder-width apart
- Lower hips back and down until thighs parallel (or lower)
- Equipment: None
- Prerequisites: 3x12 box squats
- Safety: Knees track over toes (don't cave inward), heels stay down
- Common mistakes: Incomplete depth, knees caving, excessive forward lean
- Time to master: 4-6 weeks

**Bulgarian Split Squats (Diff 6)**
- Front foot on ground, rear foot elevated on bench behind
- Lower into deep lunge (back knee toward floor)
- Equipment: Bench, box, or chair (12-18" height)
- Prerequisites: 3x12 bodyweight squats, 3x10 split squats
- Safety: Front shin vertical (knee doesn't shoot forward)
- **One of the best leg-building exercises**
- Time to master: 8-12 weeks

**Pistol Squats (Diff 9)**
- Stand on one leg, other leg extended forward
- Squat down on single leg, maintain extended leg parallel to ground
- Equipment: None (assistance for learning)
- Prerequisites: 3x8 single-leg box squats
- Safety: Requires ankle mobility, knee stability, and exceptional balance
- Not necessary for strength/hypertrophy (Bulgarian split squats equally effective)
- Time to master: 12+ weeks from single-leg box squats

### Nordic Curl Progression (Hamstring Focus)

```
Glute-Ham Raise Negatives (machine-assisted) (Diff 5)
    ↓ OR start with Nordic Curls
Nordic Curl Negatives (eccentric-only) (Diff 6)
    ↓ 3x5 reps, 6-8 weeks
Band-Assisted Nordic Curls (Diff 7)
    ↓ 3x6 reps, 8-12 weeks
Nordic Curls - Partial ROM (Diff 8)
    ↓ 3x5 reps, 12-16 weeks
Full Nordic Curls (Diff 9)
    ↓ 3x8 reps, elite level
Weighted Nordic Curls (Diff 10)
    ↓ Elite level
```

**Key Exercise Details:**

**Hip Hinge Wall Drill (Diff 1)**
- Stand 6" from wall facing away
- Push hips back to touch wall, slight knee bend
- Keep back flat (not rounded)
- Equipment: Wall
- Prerequisites: None
- Safety: Teaches fundamental pattern, prevents lower back rounding
- Time to master: 1-2 weeks

**Glute Bridges (Diff 2)**
- Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat hip-width apart
- Drive through heels, lift hips to ceiling
- Squeeze glutes hard at top
- Equipment: None
- Prerequisites: None
- Safety: Neutral spine (don't hyperextend lower back at top)
- Time to master: 3-4 weeks

**Hip Thrusts (Diff 5)**
- Upper back on bench, knees bent, feet flat
- Drive hips upward until thighs parallel to ground
- Equipment: Bench or sturdy box
- Prerequisites: 3x15 glute bridges
- Safety: Chin tucked (don't hyperextend neck looking up)
- **Best exercise for glute hypertrophy**
- Time to master: 4-6 weeks

**Nordic Curl Negatives (Diff 6)**
- Kneel with ankles anchored (under couch, partner holds, or Nordic curl station)
- Body forms straight line from knees to head
- Lower torso slowly toward ground (5+ seconds)
- Catch with hands before hitting floor
- Equipment: Ankle anchor point
- Prerequisites: 3x12 single-leg bridges
- Safety: Eccentric-only = extreme hamstring stress, DOMS is severe
- **Prevents hamstring injuries, builds tremendous strength**
- Time to master: 6-8 weeks for controlled negatives

**Full Nordic Curls (Diff 9)**
- Same as negatives but pull yourself back up using hamstrings
- Equipment: Ankle anchor
- Prerequisites: 3x5 band-assisted Nordic curls
- Safety: One of the hardest bodyweight exercises
- Time to master: 12-16+ weeks from negatives

**Key Exercise Details:**

**Dead Bugs (Diff 2)**
- Lie on back, arms toward ceiling, knees bent 90°
- Lower opposite arm overhead and opposite leg toward floor simultaneously
- Keep lower back pressed to floor
- Equipment: None
- Prerequisites: None
- Safety: **Cervical-safe (head supported)**, excellent for beginners
- Time to master: 2-3 weeks

**Bird Dogs (Diff 3)**
- Hands and knees (quadruped)
- Extend opposite arm forward and opposite leg back
- Hold 5-10 seconds, keep hips level
- Equipment: None
- Prerequisites: 3x10 dead bugs
- Safety: Look at floor (neutral neck), don't let hips rotate
- Time to master: 3-4 weeks

**Forearm Planks (Diff 4)**
- Forearms on floor, elbows under shoulders
- Body straight from head to heels
- Hold 30-60+ seconds
- Equipment: None
- Prerequisites: 3x45 sec knee planks
- Safety: Gaze between hands (neutral neck), don't let hips sag
- Breathe normally (don't hold breath)
- Time to master: 4-6 weeks

**Copenhagen Planks (Diff 7)**
- Side plank with top leg elevated on bench
- Bottom leg extended (off ground)
- Adductor strengthening
- Equipment: Bench or box
- Prerequisites: 3x45 sec side planks
- Safety: **Prevents groin injuries**, high adductor stress
- Used by professional soccer teams
- Time to master: 8-12 weeks

## 8. Anti-Rotation {#anti-rotation}

### Pallof Press Progression (Band/Cable)

```
Half-Kneeling Pallof Press (Diff 4)
    ↓ 3x12 per side, 4-6 weeks
```

**Safety Notes for Plyometrics:**
- **Landing mechanics critical:** Land softly (ball of foot first, heel follows)
- **Surface:** Use shock-absorbing surface (grass, rubber, wood - not concrete)
- **Volume:** Keep total foot contacts under 80-100 per session (beginners: 40-60)
- **Frequency:** 2x per week maximum, not on consecutive days
- **Prerequisites:** Must have strength base (3x20 bodyweight squats minimum)
- **Contraindications:** Joint issues, high body weight, poor landing mechanics

**Key Exercises:**

**Squat Jumps (Diff 4)**
- Squat down, explode upward jumping high
- Land softly, absorb impact
- Equipment: None
- Prerequisites: 3x15 bodyweight squats
- Safety: Soft landing (bend knees), quality over quantity
- Time to master: 4-6 weeks

**Box Jumps (Diff 5-7)**
- Stand facing box, jump onto box, step down
- Equipment: Sturdy box/platform (12-36" depending on level)
- Prerequisites: Squat jumps mastered
- Safety: **Step down, don't jump down** (reduces impact)
- Common mistakes: Excessive height (ego lifting), jumping down
- Time to master: 6-12 weeks depending on height

**Safety for Handstands:**
- **Cervical contraindication:** Wall handstand push-ups place load on neck
- **Wrist preparation essential:** Wrist push-ups, stretches before handstand work
- **Progression speed:** Rushing = wrist/shoulder injury
- **Practice frequency:** Daily practice (5-10 min) more effective than infrequent long sessions
- **Falling safely:** Learn to cartwheel out or pirouette out

**Key Skills:**

**Stomach-to-Wall Handstand (Diff 6)**
- Face wall, kick up to handstand (stomach faces wall)
- Heels lightly touch wall
- Equipment: Wall, open space
- Prerequisites: 3x30 sec chest-to-wall handstand
- Safety: Learn to bail (cartwheel out)
- Time to master: 8-12 weeks

**Freestanding Handstand 30 sec (Diff 10)**
- Handstand in open space, no wall support
- Hold 30+ seconds with minimal movement
- Equipment: None (mats for practice)
- Prerequisites: 3x60 sec wall handstand, balance practice
- Safety: Soft surface for learning
- Time to master: 12-24 weeks of focused practice
- **Impressive skill milestone**

## APPENDIX: Cross-Reference Guide

### Goal-Based Exercise Selection

**Fat Loss Goals:**
- Primary Patterns: All (metabolic demand)
- Add: Plyometrics (calorie burn), Locomotion (conditioning), Burpees

**Muscle Gain Goals:**
- Primary Patterns: Focus on progressive overload
- Add: Eccentric emphasis, tempo variations, weighted progressions

**Strength Goals:**
- Primary Patterns: Low rep ranges (5-8 reps)
- Add: Static Holds (L-sits), Advanced progressions

**Skill Mastery Goals:**
- Primary Patterns: Foundation
- Add: Handstands, Muscle-ups, Planche/Lever progressions

**Athletic Performance:**
- All categories
- Emphasis: Plyometrics, Ballistic, Locomotion, Rotation

**Injury Prevention/Rehab:**
- Primary Patterns: Beginner progressions
- Add: Mobility, Stability, Auxiliary patterns
- Avoid: High-impact plyometrics until cleared

### Equipment-Based Filter

**No Equipment:**
- All Primary Patterns (except dips need parallel bars)
- Secondary: Locomotion, some Rotation
- Skill-Based: Most static holds (floor L-sit, planche progressions)
- Auxiliary: Most mobility and balance work

**Pull-up Bar Only:**
- All Vertical Pull progressions
- Front Lever, Back Lever
- Muscle-ups
- Grip work (dead hangs)

**Pull-up Bar + Dip Bars:**
- Complete Primary Patterns
- L-sits, V-sits (on bars)
- Muscle-ups
- Advanced skills

**Resistance Bands:**
- Assisted progressions for Pull-ups, Dips, Muscle-ups
- Pallof Press (anti-rotation)
- Shoulder mobility work

**Rings (Gymnastic Rings):**
- All pushing and pulling patterns (increased difficulty)
- Ultimate instability training
- Ring muscle-ups
- Skin-the-cat, inversions
